About Me

I am a Postdoctoral Research Associate at the University of Virginia, specializing in the intersection of artificial intelligence and climate science. My research leverages generative AI, deep learning, and physics-informed models to address critical challenges in climate prediction and adaptation. I focus on developing novel methodologies for precipitation downscaling, bias correction, and quantifying hydroclimatic risks in a changing climate.

My work bridges computational innovation with environmental sustainability, aiming to provide actionable insights for climate resilience planning and disaster risk reduction. I collaborate with interdisciplinary teams to translate cutting-edge AI research into practical solutions for water resources management and climate adaptation strategies.
